Portfolio Artifact 2

Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning Case Study

Choosing the right AI approach for the right problem

This artifact compares traditional machine learning and deep learning through real-world business and technology examples. It goes beyond definitions by showing how data type, explainability, complexity, and computing needs affect the decision.

ML vs DL
Machine Learning

Best for structured data, clear features, faster training, and explainable decisions.

Deep Learning

Best for complex raw data like images, video, speech, and large-scale patterns.

Professional Skill Shown

Ability to compare technologies and select the right solution for the problem.

Traditional Machine Learning

Customer Churn Prediction

A telecom company can use machine learning to predict which customers may cancel their service. The model can analyze structured data such as contract length, monthly charges, service complaints, payment history, plan type, and customer support interactions.

Why ML Fits This Problem

Machine learning is suitable because the important information can be organized into clear features. A model such as logistic regression, decision tree, random forest, or support vector machine can learn from historical customer behavior and identify customers who may be at risk of leaving.

Why Deep Learning Is Not the Best Fit

Deep learning would add unnecessary complexity for this type of structured business data. It may require more data, more computing power, and more training time. It may also be harder for managers to understand why a customer is flagged as high risk.

Key takeaway: ML is strong when the problem needs clear, explainable predictions from organized data.
Deep Learning

Autonomous Driving

Autonomous driving systems use deep learning to process camera images, sensor data, road signs, traffic lights, lane markings, pedestrians, vehicles, and unexpected road conditions in real time.

Why DL Fits This Problem

Deep learning is suitable because the data is complex and unstructured. A self-driving system cannot rely only on manually selected features. Deep neural networks can automatically learn visual patterns from large amounts of image, video, and sensor data.

Why Traditional ML Is Not Enough

Traditional machine learning would struggle because there are too many possible driving conditions to manually define. Lighting, weather, traffic behavior, construction zones, pedestrians, and road shapes create patterns that are too complex for simple feature-based models.

Key takeaway: DL is strong when the system must learn from raw, complex, high-volume data.

Decision Guide: When Would I Choose Each?

Choose Machine Learning When...

  • The data is structured in tables or records.
  • The features are clear and understandable.
  • The organization needs explainable results.
  • The project needs faster training and lower cost.

Choose Deep Learning When...

  • The data includes images, video, audio, or text.
  • The patterns are too complex to define manually.
  • There is a large amount of training data.
  • The task requires automatic feature learning.

Title: What Is It?

This artifact is a real-world case study that compares machine learning and deep learning. It explains how each approach works, where each one is useful, and why choosing the correct approach matters in AI/ML projects.

Portfolio Artifact 1

AI and ML Timeline

Title: What Is It?

This artifact is a visual timeline showing the evolution of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning from the 1950s to today. It includes major milestones such as the Turing Test, Dartmouth Conference, AI winters, Deep Blue, AlexNet, AlphaGo, Transformer models, ChatGPT, and the modern multimodal AI era.

Objective

I created this artifact to understand how AI developed over time and how historical events shaped the field. The goal was to show the connection between early AI ideas, periods of slowed progress, and recent breakthroughs made possible by deep learning, GPUs, cloud computing, and large datasets.

Process

I reviewed the assignment instructions, researched important AI milestones, organized the events in chronological order, and grouped them into major sections such as foundations, AI winters, deep learning, and generative AI. I designed the timeline to be simple, visual, and easy to understand.

Tools Used

I used ChatGPT to organize ideas and improve wording, Canva or Lucidchart to design the timeline, course materials for assignment guidance, and GitHub Pages to publish this portfolio website.

Value Proposition

This artifact is important because it shows my ability to research, organize, and explain complex technical information in a clear visual format. Professionally, it demonstrates skills in research, communication, technology awareness, design thinking, and digital presentation.

Reflection

What I Learned From My Artifacts

Creating the AI and ML Timeline helped me understand that artificial intelligence did not develop overnight. Before this assignment, I mostly thought of AI as a modern technology, but this artifact showed me that AI has a long history going back to the 1950s.

One important lesson I learned is that AI progress depends on more than strong ideas. Early researchers had big goals, but the technology was not always ready. The AI winters showed that limited computing power, high expectations, and lack of practical results slowed progress.

My second artifact, the Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning case study, helped me show a different skill. Instead of creating a visual history timeline, I compared two AI/ML approaches and explained why each one fits a different type of problem.

Together, these artifacts show both my understanding of AI history and my ability to apply AI/ML concepts to real-world decision-making. They also show my growth in research, communication, critical thinking, and digital presentation.
